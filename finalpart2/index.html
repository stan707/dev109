Final Part 2: Portfolio Reflection

What was your previous knowledge of HTML/CSS/JavaScript?

  • I had a basic understanding of HTML, knowing how to structure a webpage with elements like headings, paragraphs, and links.
  • For CSS, I had some experience with styling, such as changing colors and fonts, and using classes and IDs for layout design.
  • I had very little knowledge of JavaScript, mainly understanding the basic concepts like variables and functions, but had not worked much with it before.

Did you use a template or did you build your portfolio from scratch and why?

  • I decided to build my portfolio from scratch. I wanted to challenge myself and learn more about the web development process, especially layout and responsive design.
  • While templates can be a time-saver, I felt that building my portfolio from scratch would give me a better understanding of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

What was your most challenging part of your portfolio (i.e., the template, artifacts, CSS, etc.)?

  • The most challenging part was CSS, especially when it came to positioning and making the layout responsive. Understanding how different CSS properties interact with each other was tricky at times.
  • Another challenge was integrating JavaScript for interactive elements, as I had limited experience with scripting before this project.

How did this class change your view on Web Development?

  • This class helped me realize the complexity and creativity involved in web development. It's not just about coding; it's also about designing and creating user-friendly, responsive experiences.
  • I now see web development as an iterative process where you constantly improve and refine your work. I also gained an appreciation for the importance of accessibility and performance.

What would you recommend to future students taking this class?

  • Start with a solid foundation of HTML and CSS before diving into JavaScript, as it will make learning more advanced concepts easier.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment and break things. It's all part of the learning process, and debugging is a key skill in web development.
  • Use developer tools in your browser frequently to troubleshoot and fine-tune your code.
  • Take your time with each project and don't rush. The more effort you put into your portfolio, the more it will reflect your growth as a developer.
